GoodSeed

Privacy Policy

Effective July 13, 2026

The short version: GoodSeed collects the minimum needed to run a family chore-and-rewards app. We don't run ads, we don't use tracking or analytics, we never sell data, and parents can delete everything at any time. Kids can't sign up on their own — a parent always creates and controls the family.

What GoodSeed is

GoodSeed is a faith-based family chore & rewards web app. Parents create a family, add child profiles, assign tasks, and approve rewards. This policy explains what information we handle, why, and the choices you have. It applies to the app and the website at goodseed-family.netlify.app.

Information we collect

From parents

  • Your name and an optional email address.
  • A family name and an invite code used to connect your family's devices.
  • If you subscribe to GoodSeed Plus: payment is processed by Stripe. We never see or store your card number — Stripe sends us only your subscription status.
  • If you join the Teams & Classrooms waitlist: the email address you submit.

About children (added by a parent)

  • A first name or nickname and an optional age.
  • An avatar — an emoji, or an optional photo if the parent chooses to add one.
  • App activity: tasks completed, Seeds earned and spent, streaks, levels, and badges.

What we do NOT collect

  • No child email addresses, phone numbers, or passwords — children sign in with the family code.
  • No location data, contact lists, or browsing history.
  • No advertising identifiers, analytics trackers, or third-party cookies. There are no ads in GoodSeed.

Where your data lives

  • On your device. GoodSeed is local-first: your family's data is stored in your browser on your own device.
  • In our database (Plus families). If you subscribe to Plus, your family's data syncs through our database (hosted by Supabase in the United States) so every family member's device stays up to date. Access is restricted so only devices that have joined your family with your invite code can read your family's data.

How we use information

Only to provide GoodSeed: running the app, syncing your family's devices, processing Plus subscriptions, and responding when you contact us. We do not sell personal information, share it for advertising, or use it to build profiles. Ever.

Children's privacy (COPPA)

GoodSeed is designed for families with children, so we take the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act seriously:

  • Parental consent is built into the design. A child cannot create an account. Only a parent can create a family and add a child profile, and only a parent can share the family code that lets a child's device join.
  • We collect the minimum. A child's profile needs only a first name or nickname. Age and photo are optional and added only by a parent.
  • No ads or tracking for anyone, children included.
  • Parents can review and delete. You can view and edit every child profile in the app, delete an individual child profile, or delete the entire family — which permanently removes your family's data from the device and from our database.
  • Third parties are limited to infrastructure: Supabase (database hosting), Netlify (website hosting), and Stripe (parent payments only). None of them receive children's data for their own use.

Data retention and deletion

  • Your family's data is kept while your family uses GoodSeed.
  • Deleting a child profile removes that child's data. Deleting the family (Settings → Delete family) permanently removes your family's data from your device and our database.
  • Routine database backups age out automatically within approximately 7 days.
  • Subscription and payment records are retained by Stripe as required for financial and legal record-keeping.
  • Waitlist emails are kept until the feature launches or you ask us to remove yours.

Security

Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest. Database access is controlled by per-family membership rules enforced by the database itself, so one family can never read another family's data.

Changes to this policy

If we make meaningful changes — for example, when GoodSeed for Teams & Classrooms launches — we'll update this page and the effective date, and note the change in the app.
